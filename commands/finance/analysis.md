You are performing a financial analysis. Follow these instructions precisely.

## Format

Structure the financial analysis as follows:

1. **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
   - Subject of analysis (company, division, project, investment)
   - Period of analysis
   - Key findings (3-5 bullet points)
   - Overall financial health assessment
   - Primary recommendations

2. **FINANCIAL STATEMENTS ANALYSIS**

   **Income Statement Analysis**
   - Revenue trends and composition
   - Cost structure analysis (fixed vs. variable)
   - Margin analysis (gross, operating, net)
   - Year-over-year and period-over-period changes
   - Common-size analysis (each line as % of revenue)

   **Balance Sheet Analysis**
   - Asset composition and quality
   - Liability structure and maturity profile
   - Equity analysis
   - Common-size analysis (each line as % of total assets)

   **Cash Flow Analysis**
   - Operating cash flow quality and trends
   - Capital expenditure analysis
   - Free cash flow calculation and trends
   - Financing activities and capital structure changes
   - Cash conversion cycle

3. **RATIO ANALYSIS**

   Present ratios in a structured table format with comparative periods:

   **Liquidity Ratios**
   - Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
   - Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
   - Cash Ratio = Cash & Equivalents / Current Liabilities
   - Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities

   **Profitability Ratios**
   - Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Revenue
   - Operating Margin = Operating Income / Revenue
   - Net Margin = Net Income / Revenue
   - Return on Assets (ROA) = Net Income / Total Assets
   - Return on Equity (ROE) = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity
   - Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) = NOPAT / Invested Capital

   **Efficiency Ratios**
   - Asset Turnover = Revenue / Total Assets
   - Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory
   - Receivables Turnover = Revenue / Average Accounts Receivable
   - Days Sales Outstanding = 365 / Receivables Turnover
   - Days Inventory Outstanding = 365 / Inventory Turnover
   - Days Payable Outstanding = 365 / (COGS / Average Accounts Payable)

   **Leverage Ratios**
   - Debt-to-Equity = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
   - Debt-to-Assets = Total Debt / Total Assets
   - Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense
   - Debt Service Coverage = EBITDA / Total Debt Service

   **Valuation Ratios** (if publicly traded)
   - Price-to-Earnings (P/E)
   - Price-to-Book (P/B)
   - Enterprise Value / EBITDA (EV/EBITDA)
   - Price-to-Sales (P/S)

4. **TREND ANALYSIS**
   - Identify significant trends across 3-5 periods minimum
   - Calculate compound annual growth rates (CAGR) for key metrics
   - Highlight inflection points or anomalies
   - Discuss underlying drivers of trends

5. **BENCHMARKING**
   - Compare key metrics against industry averages or peers
   - Identify areas of outperformance and underperformance
   - Note any structural differences that affect comparability
   - Provide context for deviations from benchmarks

6. **DUPONT ANALYSIS** (if applicable)
   - Decompose ROE: Net Margin x Asset Turnover x Equity Multiplier
   - Identify which component drives ROE performance
   - Compare DuPont components against peers

7. **RISK ASSESSMENT**
   - Financial risk factors (liquidity, solvency, concentration)
   - Operational risk factors
   - Market and industry risk factors
   - Regulatory and compliance risk factors

8. **FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS**
   - Summarize key strengths and weaknesses
   - Provide specific, actionable recommendations
   - Prioritize recommendations by impact and urgency
   - Identify areas requiring further investigation

## Formatting Guidelines

- Use consistent number formatting: commas for thousands, two decimal places for percentages and ratios.
- Clearly label all currency units.
- Use parentheses for negative numbers: $(1,234).
- Present comparative data side-by-side in tables.
- Include period-over-period change (absolute and percentage).
- Use color coding or symbols to flag positive/negative trends where possible (use arrows or +/- indicators in text).
- Round appropriately: dollars to nearest thousand or million for large figures, ratios to two decimal places.
