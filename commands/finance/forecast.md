## Format

Structure the financial forecast as follows:

1. **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
   - Forecast period and scope
   - Key assumptions summary
   - Headline projections (revenue, expenses, net income)
   - Key risks and opportunities

2. **ASSUMPTIONS AND METHODOLOGY**
   - **Revenue Assumptions**:
     - Growth rate basis (historical trend, market analysis, pipeline)
     - Pricing assumptions
     - Volume/unit assumptions
     - Seasonality factors
     - New product/service contributions
   - **Expense Assumptions**:
     - Fixed vs. variable cost classification
     - Headcount and compensation assumptions
     - Inflation and escalation factors
     - Capital expenditure plans
   - **Methodology**: State the forecasting method used (bottom-up, top-down, hybrid, trend extrapolation, regression, etc.)

3. **INCOME STATEMENT FORECAST**
   - Present monthly or quarterly projections for the forecast period.
   - Use this structure:
     ```
     Revenue
       - Product/Service Line A
       - Product/Service Line B
       - Other Revenue
     Total Revenue

     Cost of Goods Sold / Cost of Revenue
     Gross Profit
     Gross Margin %

     Operating Expenses
       - Salaries & Benefits
       - Rent & Facilities
       - Marketing & Sales
       - Technology & Infrastructure
       - General & Administrative
       - Depreciation & Amortization
     Total Operating Expenses

     Operating Income (EBIT)
     Operating Margin %

     Interest & Other Income/Expense
     Income Before Tax
     Tax Provision
     Net Income
     Net Margin %
     ```

4. **BALANCE SHEET FORECAST** (if applicable)
   - Key asset and liability projections
   - Working capital changes
   - Debt schedule

5. **CASH FLOW FORECAST**
   - Operating cash flow
   - Investing cash flow
   - Financing cash flow
   - Net change in cash
   - Ending cash position

6. **KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS**
   - Revenue growth rate (YoY, QoQ)
   - Gross margin
   - Operating margin
   - Net margin
   - EBITDA and EBITDA margin
   - Cash conversion ratio
   - Customer metrics (if applicable): CAC, LTV, churn
   - Industry-specific KPIs as relevant

7. **SCENARIO ANALYSIS**
   - **Base Case**: Most likely outcome based on stated assumptions
   - **Upside Case**: Optimistic scenario with identified drivers
   - **Downside Case**: Pessimistic scenario with identified risks
   - Quantify the impact of each scenario on key metrics
   - Identify the key variables that drive scenario variance

8. **SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS**
   - Identify the top 3-5 variables with the greatest impact on outcomes
   - Show the effect of +/- changes in each variable on net income and cash flow
   - Present as a sensitivity table where possible

9. **RISKS AND MITIGANTS**
   - List key risks to the forecast
   - Quantify potential impact where possible
   - Identify mitigating actions or strategies

## Formatting Guidelines

- Use consistent number formatting: commas for thousands, two decimal places for percentages.
- Clearly label all currency units (USD, EUR, etc.).
- Use parentheses for negative numbers: $(1,234).
- Right-align all numerical columns.
- Bold totals and subtotals.
- Use horizontal rules to separate major sections in tables.
- Include period-over-period comparisons where meaningful.
- Label all assumptions with their source or basis.

## Disclaimer

Always include the following disclaimer at the end:

> **DISCLAIMER**: This financial forecast was generated with AI assistance and is intended for planning and discussion purposes only. Projections are based on the stated assumptions and are inherently uncertain. Actual results may differ materially from these projections due to market conditions, economic factors, competitive dynamics, and other variables beyond the scope of this forecast. This document does not constitute financial advice, an investment recommendation, or a guarantee of future performance. A qualified financial professional should review all assumptions, methodologies, and projections before any business decisions are made based on this forecast.
